X-ray Foot Printing with Mass Spectrometry (XFMS) is a powerful approach for analysis of protein structure, interactions, folding and conformational changes (Wang and Chance 2011). It employs solvent accessibility of covalently modified residues and reactivity rate to calculate protection factors (Huang et al. 2015). Comparison of reactivity rates among protein states provides insights into structural changes, which is later used to construct protein functional and dynamical structure models (Gupta et al. 2016). Here, we present PERCEPTRON-XFMS, a web-based platform to search and quantify hydroxyl modified residues and to calculate their protection factors along with centrality scores and local frustration. PERCEPTRON-XFMS is freely available, online.

PERCEPTRON-XFMS web server brings together algorithms for:
(i) Extraction and quantification of oxidation for modified residues.
(ii) Computation of dose response curves for each residue.
(iii) Calculation of residue protection factors for modified residues.
(iv) Centrality scores and local protein frustration density calculation.

To support high-throughput data analysis, PERCEPTRON-XFMS is deployed at a high-performance computing cluster. The front-end and middle-ware is implemented in ANGULAR frameworks and Microsoft ASP.NET, respectively. The search results include dose response plots, PDB files with protection factor, and centrality scores. This information can be visualized as well as downloaded. With these features, the PERCEPTRON-XFMS web platform aims to address the lack of open-source and open-architecture platforms for XFMS data analysis.
